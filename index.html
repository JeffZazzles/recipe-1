Roasted Cherry Tomato Chicken Salad

A fresh, crunchy chicken salad with juicy roasted cherry tomatoes, sweet corn, gherkins, and a creamy Dijon yogurt dressing. The roasted tomato oil gets folded in at the end, turning the whole bowl glossy, savory, and extremely shareable.

Prep15 min
Cook12 min
Total27 min
Serves2 to 4
Make the salad
creamy Dijon roasted tomatoes pickle crunch

Why it works

The hot tray does the heavy lifting: cherry tomatoes collapse into sweet, garlicky little flavor bombs, then their oil becomes part of the dressing. Gherkins bring crunch, corn brings sweetness, chicken makes it a meal, and the yogurt-Dijon dressing keeps everything bright instead of heavy.

The secret ingredient is the tomato tray oil. It’s basically a tiny roasted-garlic sunbeam.

Best eaten after the leaves have had a few minutes to soak up the dressing.

Ingredients

For the roasted tomatoes

  • Cherry tomatoes, as many as you like, then add a few more
  • Avocado oil
  • Garlic salt
  • Black pepper
  • A few garlic cloves, peeled

For the salad

  • 1 bag salad leaves, torn into smaller pieces
  • 3 to 4 gherkins, thinly sliced, still thick enough to keep some crunch
  • 1 can corn, drained
  • 1 to 2 cooked chicken breasts, sliced or shredded

For the dressing

  • 135 g Greek yogurt
  • ½ tablespoon Dijon mustard, or more to taste
  • Black pepper
  • A squeeze of lemon, lime, or calamansi juice
  • A pinch of paprika
  • Optional: fresh garlic or the roasted garlic from the tomatoes

Instructions

  1. Roast the tomatoes.

    Preheat the oven to 180°C. Add the cherry tomatoes to a baking dish with avocado oil, garlic salt, black pepper, and a few garlic cloves. Mix well so the tomatoes are fully coated.

  2. Bake.

    Roast the tomatoes for about 12 minutes, until they soften and release some juices. Set aside to cool slightly.

  3. Make the dressing.

    In a bowl, mix the Greek yogurt with Dijon mustard, black pepper, citrus juice, and paprika. Taste and adjust the mustard or citrus juice if needed.

  4. Add garlic to the dressing.

    Mash in one of the roasted garlic cloves from the tomato tray for a softer flavor, or add a little fresh garlic for a sharper, spicier taste.

    Recommended: add both, but go easy on the fresh garlic if romance is expected to commence later.

  5. Assemble the salad.

    Add the salad leaves, corn, gherkins, and chicken to a large bowl. Try to slice the chicken so it is roughly the same thickness as the gherkins.

  6. Finish.

    Add the roasted tomatoes to the salad. Pour in the dressing, along with some of the oil and juices from the tomato tray. Toss everything together.

  7. Let it sit.

    Leave the salad for a few minutes before serving so the dressing can soak into the leaves a little.

Notes

For sharpness

Add a little more Dijon mustard. Start small and sneak up on it.

For brightness

Add more citrus juice. Lemon, lime, or calamansi all work beautifully.

For smoke

Add a pinch more paprika. The salad gets deeper without becoming heavy.

Made for sharing, printing, and sending to anyone who needs a salad with main-character energy.
